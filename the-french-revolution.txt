The French Revolution (1789-1799)

Description: 
The French Revolution was a period of radical political and social upheaval in France during the late 18th century. 
It saw the overthrow of the monarchy, the rise of a republic, 
and eventually led to the Reign of Terror, 
followed by the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte.

Additional Info: 
The causes of the French Revolution included socio-economic inequality, 
political discontent, and Enlightenment ideas promoting democracy and individual rights.
The revolution led to the end of feudalism, the spread of revolutionary ideals, 
and the transformation of France's political landscape. 
Solutions included the establishment of new political institutions 
and the spread of revolutionary ideals across Europe.
