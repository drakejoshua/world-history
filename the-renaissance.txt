The Renaissance (14th-17th centuries)

Description: 
The Renaissance was a cultural and intellectual movement that spanned the 14th to the 17th centuries, 
marking a period of significant advancements in art, science, literature, and philosophy.

Additional Info: 
The Renaissance emerged in Italy and spread across Europe, 
fueled by renewed interest in classical knowledge, humanism, 
and the patronage of wealthy individuals and institutions. 
It witnessed groundbreaking achievements in art by masters such as Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo, 
scientific advancements by figures like Galileo Galilei, 
and a shift towards human-centric perspectives. 
The Renaissance contributed to the Enlightenment and the shaping of modern society.
